# JNY-XXX — <title>

> A journey is the **why**. No implementation detail — no file names, components, APIs, or
> libraries. If you're naming a function, you're writing a spec.

## Persona

Who is this person? Be concrete and contextual — role, what they're doing, their relationship
to accessibility. (e.g. "a sighted front-end developer shipping a public form for the European
Commission who has never used a screen reader and has no AT installed".)

## Trigger

The moment they reach for this tool. What just happened?

## Goal

The outcome they want, in their own words.

## Story

A short narrative of the journey from trigger to goal — what they try, what they expect to
see/hear, how they know they've succeeded.

## Success criteria

Observable, testable signals that the journey succeeded. (e.g. "they can see, at a glance,
which headings a screen reader would skip".)

## Failure modes

What could go wrong *for them* (confusion, false confidence, friction). Not bugs — user-level
failure.

## Out of scope

What this journey explicitly does **not** cover.

Anything that needs a decision before a spec can be written.
